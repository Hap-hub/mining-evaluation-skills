# Lithium — Evaluation Notes

**Deposit types**: hard-rock spodumene pegmatites (Australia, Canada — mined conventionally, produces spodumene concentrate), brine deposits (Chile/Argentina "Lithium Triangle" salars — evaporation ponds, long lead times but low opex), and emerging clay-hosted / sedimentary deposits (e.g., some US projects) with less-proven commercial processing routes.

**Grade reporting is unit-sensitive — check units carefully.** Hard-rock resources are commonly reported as % Li2O; brines are reported as mg/L (or ppm) lithium in solution. These are not directly comparable, and conversions between Li2O, Li2CO3 equivalent (LCE), and contained lithium metal are a common source of confusion or (occasionally) misleading marketing in project decks — always confirm what unit and what compound basis (Li2O vs Li2CO3e vs Li metal) any grade or production figure is quoted in before comparing across projects.

**Processing routes differ enormously by deposit type**:
- Hard rock: crush/flotation → spodumene concentrate (~6% Li2O) → sold to converters, or increasingly integrated into concentrate-to-chemical (lithium hydroxide/carbonate) conversion plants, which add significant capex but capture more value.
- Brine: extraction via evaporation ponds (long lead time, 12–18+ months residence time, weather-dependent) or emerging Direct Lithium Extraction (DLE) technologies, which claim faster/higher-recovery extraction but are less commercially proven at scale — treat DLE-based project economics with extra scrutiny on technology risk until there's operating-scale proof, not just pilot results.

**Market factors**: lithium pricing has been unusually volatile (large swings up and down over the past several years tied to EV demand cycles and Chinese supply/demand dynamics) — a project's price deck should be checked against recent spot/contract pricing via search rather than assumed from memory, since this market moves fast. Also check whether pricing is for spodumene concentrate, lithium carbonate, or lithium hydroxide — these track differently and battery-grade specifications carry premiums.

**Common evaluation pitfalls**: conflating LCE (lithium carbonate equivalent) production capacity with contained lithium in the resource without clarifying the conversion basis; brine projects with short pilot-scale test data extrapolated to full-scale recovery/production assumptions without adequate hydrogeological/pumping test support.
