# Copper — Evaluation Notes

**Deposit types**: porphyry copper (by far the largest source globally — Chile, Peru dominate), sediment-hosted (Zambia/DRC Copperbelt), volcanogenic massive sulphide (VMS, often polymetallic with Zn/Pb/Au/Ag), IOCG (iron oxide copper gold, e.g., Olympic Dam-style).

**Typical grades**: porphyry deposits are large-tonnage, low-grade (~0.3–1.0% Cu is common and economic at scale due to enormous tonnage — often billions of tonnes); sediment-hosted and VMS deposits can run notably higher grade (1–4%+ Cu) at smaller tonnage.

**Cut-off grade context**: because porphyries are typically open-pit, large-scale, low-grade, mining/processing cost per tonne is the dominant driver of cut-off — small cost or price swings move enormous tonnage in/out of the resource, so cut-off sensitivity analysis is unusually important for this commodity.

**Processing**: sulphide ore → flotation to concentrate (~25–30% Cu) → smelting/refining; oxide ore → heap leach/SX-EW (produces cathode copper directly, bypassing smelting — relevant to capex and market flexibility). Check which route a project assumes; SX-EW projects have materially different capex/opex profiles and no smelter/TC-RC exposure.

**Market factors to weigh**: TC/RCs (treatment and refining charges) paid to smelters materially affect concentrate producer economics and have been volatile; by-product credits (Au, Ag, Mo are common porphyry by-products) can significantly improve project economics and should be checked in any cash-flow model. Copper demand is heavily tied to electrification/grid/EV narratives — useful context but don't treat forecast demand growth as a substitute for the study's actual contracted/assumed price deck.

**Common evaluation pitfalls**: porphyry projects are large-capex, long-permitting-timeline projects — a PEA showing a fast, cheap build for a large low-grade porphyry deposit is a red flag worth probing (compare capex intensity, $/tonne of throughput capacity, against known peer projects).
